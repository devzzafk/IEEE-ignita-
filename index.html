About IEEE & this event

01 · The institute

What is IEEE?

The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) is the world's largest technical professional organisation, advancing innovation in electronics, computing and engineering for the benefit of humanity through publications, conferences and a global community of student and professional members.

02 · Our branch

IEEE Student Branch, LBSITW

The IEEE Student Branch at LBS Institute of Technology for Women gives students a space to learn beyond the syllabus — through workshops, technical talks, project showcases and a peer network that carries into their careers.

03 · Why Ignite

The objective of Ignite

Ignite exists to close the gap between classroom theory and real-world readiness — one day of workshops, career sessions and competitive challenges built to spark new skills, sharpen the ones you already have, and connect you with people building the same future.

Frequently asked questions

Ignite is open to all students, regardless of department or year — you don't need to be an IEEE member or an engineering student to attend.

Entry to the event is free for IEEE members. Non-members can register for a small nominal fee, payable online or at the registration desk.

One registration covers entry to all sessions and challenges throughout the day. Seats in hands-on workshops are limited, so arrive a little early.

Yes — every participant receives a digital certificate of participation, and winners of competitive events receive certificates of merit plus prizes.

For the Innovation Pitch and Problem-Solving Challenge, teams of up to four members are welcome, and members may come from different institutions.

Bring a valid college ID, your registration confirmation, and a laptop if you're attending the Web Development Workshop or Coding Challenge.
